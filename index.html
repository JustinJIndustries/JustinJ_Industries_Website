Project Patterns

Common ways JustinJ Industries helps teams clean up websites, tracking, and handoffs without adding unnecessary process.

Website Build and Cleanup

Need: A site or landing page needs clearer structure, cleaner implementation, or maintenance after a period of drift.

Support: Update page structure, front-end behavior, CMS content patterns, accessibility basics, and technical SEO details.

Deliverable: Maintainable pages with direct documentation on what changed and what should be checked next.

Analytics Tagging QA

Need: Adobe Analytics, Adobe Experience Platform, GA4, GTM, or related tracking needs review before or after a release.

Support: Inspect data layer behavior, event collection, tag firing, consent-aware conditions, naming consistency, and payloads that should avoid sensitive data.

Deliverable: A concise QA log, confirmed findings, and practical fix recommendations.

CMS and Workflow Support

Need: Small teams need repeatable page updates, clearer content ownership, or help turning messy manual work into a documented workflow.

Support: Improve WordPress or Craft CMS patterns, page components, QA checklists, and AI-assisted build or documentation workflows where appropriate.

Deliverable: Cleaner implementation and notes that make future updates easier to repeat.

Accessibility Statement

JustinJ Industries aims to build accessible, usable digital experiences using semantic HTML, keyboard-friendly interactions, readable content structure, and practical QA. Accessibility conformance should be validated for each project’s scope, content, and platform.

Privacy and Responsible Measurement

Measurement work is designed to be documented, testable, and privacy-aware. JustinJ Industries can help teams define event taxonomies, QA tagging behavior, and avoid sending sensitive form contents or unnecessary personal data into analytics platforms.

When analytics is implemented, event collection should be scoped to business outcomes and implementation QA. Contact-form message content, names, and email addresses should remain in contact systems and out of analytics event payloads.
